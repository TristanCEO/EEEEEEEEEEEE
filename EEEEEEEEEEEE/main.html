About

General Santos City, known as the "Tuna Capital of the Philippines," is a bustling city in the southern part of Mindanao. It's renowned for its rich culture, vibrant festivals, and thriving tuna industry.

Nestled in the southern part of Mindanao, General Santos City—affectionately known as GenSan—is a vibrant metropolis celebrated for its rich cultural heritage, thriving tuna industry, and warm hospitality. Whether you're an adventure seeker, a history enthusiast, or a culinary explorer, GenSan offers a diverse array of experiences to delight every traveler


Top Tourist Spots

  • General Santos City Fish Port Complex - Witness the bustling tuna auctions and fresh seafood at the largest fish port in the Philippines.
  • Plaza Heneral Santos - A 4.5-hectare park featuring the City Hall and a variety of street food vendors.
  • Mount Matutum - A majestic mountain offering hiking opportunities with breathtaking views of Sarangani Bay.
  • Queen Tuna Park - A coastal park providing beachside cabanas and shaded areas, perfect for relaxation.
  • Tiongson Arcade - A culinary hotspot offering fresh seafood and local delicacies in a vibrant setting.


Festivals

Tuna Festival - Celebrated every September 1 to 5, featuring a colorful tuna-themed float parade, culinary competitions, and seafood bazaars.

Kalilangan Festival - Held annually in February, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of GenSan through street dancing, musical performances, and traditional ceremonies.


Where to Stay

  • SM City General Santos - The largest mall in Soccsksargen, offering a variety of shopping and dining options.
  • Robinsons GenSan - A two-storey mall with over 150 shops and restaurants, providing a comprehensive retail experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit General Santos City?
The best time to visit is during the Tuna Festival in September or the Kalilangan Festival in February for a vibrant cultural experience.
How do I get to General Santos City?
You can fly directly to General Santos International Airport from Manila or Cebu, or travel by bus from major Mindanao cities.
Is General Santos City safe for tourists?
Yes, GenSan is generally safe for tourists. As with any city, exercise usual precautions and consult local authorities for updates.
What local delicacies should I try?
Don’t miss the fresh tuna sashimi, kinilaw, and other seafood dishes at local markets and restaurants!

Latest Blog

  • A Day at the Fish Port: GenSan’s Tuna Culture May 2025
    Experience the excitement of the early morning tuna auctions and discover why GenSan is the Tuna Capital of the Philippines. The Fish Port Complex is a hive of activity, with fishermen unloading their catch and buyers from all over the world bidding for the best tuna. The port not only supports the local economy but also showcases the city’s global significance in the seafood industry.
    Fun Fact: GenSan exports tons of sashimi-grade tuna to Japan and the US every year.
    Source: Philstar: GenSan, Tuna Capital of the Philippines
  • Top 5 Must-Try Foods in GenSan April 2025
    From kinilaw (Filipino ceviche) to grilled tuna jaw (panga), GenSan’s food scene is a paradise for seafood lovers. Don’t miss the crispy tuna tail, sinuglaw, and the famous tuna sashimi served fresh at local eateries and markets.
    Recommended Spot: Tiongson Arcade for a true local food adventure.
    Source: Spot.ph: GenSan Food Guide
  • Kalilangan Festival: A Celebration of Unity February 2025
    The Kalilangan Festival is a vibrant celebration of GenSan’s multicultural roots, featuring street dances, parades, and traditional music. It commemorates the harmonious coexistence of Christians, Muslims, and indigenous peoples in the city.
    Highlight: Don’t miss the colorful street dancing and cultural shows at the city plaza.
    Source: GenSan Official: Kalilangan Festival
  • Eco Adventures: Hiking Mount Matutum March 2025
    Mount Matutum offers a challenging yet rewarding trek for nature lovers. The trail winds through lush forests and ends with breathtaking views of South Cotabato and Sarangani Bay. The mountain is also home to rare flora and fauna, making it a must-visit for eco-tourists.
    Tip: Hire a local guide for a safe and informative climb.
    Source: Lakwatsero: Mount Matutum
  • Queen Tuna Park: Urban Beach Escape January 2025
    Queen Tuna Park is a favorite spot for locals and tourists alike. Enjoy a relaxing afternoon by the sea, rent a cabana, or sample street food from nearby vendors. The park is also a great place to watch the sunset over Sarangani Bay.
    Source: Choose Philippines: Queen Tuna Park

FAQ

Can I swim at Queen Tuna Park?

Yes, Queen Tuna Park has designated swimming areas and beachside cabanas for visitors.

Are there guided tours available?

Several local agencies offer guided city and food tours. Check with the tourism office for recommendations.
